Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript entitled "Cost–Resilience Thresholds for Battery–Hydrogen Hybrid Microgrids in Islanded Power Systems: An 8760-h Real-Weather Philippines Study" for publication in Applied Energy as a Full Length Research Article.

This manuscript fits the scope of Applied Energy at the intersection of microgrids and local energy systems, energy storage, hydrogen integration, and resilience-oriented energy-system decision-making. The paper addresses a narrower but practically important question than full capacity expansion: under fixed candidate portfolio capacities and real-weather hourly operation, when does hydrogen materially shift the cost–resilience frontier of an islanded microgrid relative to diesel-based flexibility?

To answer this question, the study develops an 8760-h chronological operational framework and combines it with outage-focused resilience assessment using critical-load-served ratio, expected energy not served, and survivable outage duration. A representative off-grid Philippines archetype is used to compare battery-only, diesel-battery, and battery-hydrogen portfolios. The results show that hydrogen is not a universally least-cost option. Instead, its value emerges only beyond identifiable threshold conditions shaped by outage severity, delivered diesel cost, carbon exposure, and hydrogen capital cost.

We believe the manuscript will be of interest to Applied Energy readers working on islanded power systems, long-duration storage, hydrogen-based microgrids, and resilience-oriented planning under high-risk operating conditions.

This manuscript is original, has not been published previously except as permitted by journal policy, and is not under consideration elsewhere. The submission package includes article highlights and supplementary material. A public repository containing the code, configuration files, and processed case-study datasets will be released upon acceptance.
